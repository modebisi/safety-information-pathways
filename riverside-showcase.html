The problem this solves

Small chemical-handling sites often have safety documents, but staff cannot quickly find the right approved information at the point of work.

  • SDS files in one folder
  • COSHH assessments somewhere else
  • SOPs with unclear version control
  • Inspection forms on paper or spreadsheets
  • Emergency information not linked to tasks/equipment
  • New starters overloaded with documents

The offer

Chemical Safety Hub Pilot

We organise the company’s existing approved information into a practical, mobile-friendly system. The company remains responsible for approving content and legal compliance.

Buyer outcome: workers can find the right document, procedure, chemical record or equipment information faster.

About this Concept

This fictional showcase explores a practical question:

If approved safety information already exists, can it be made easier to navigate around the way people actually work — by task, chemical, equipment and area?

The idea came from trying to improve a practical information problem encountered in chemical-industry H&S work.

  • Bring links to existing approved information into clearer pathways
  • Help users navigate by task or equipment rather than folder knowledge
  • Make document ownership, status and review needs more visible
  • Support mobile-friendly access where appropriate and controlled
Important: This showcase does not certify compliance, replace competent advice, create risk assessments, or invent site-specific procedures.

30-Day Pilot Setup

A simple delivery process that a first customer can understand.

Days 1–3 — ScopeChoose one area, 3–5 priority tasks and named customer approver.
Days 4–10 — OrganiseIndex existing approved SDS, COSHH, SOPs, emergency links and equipment records.
Days 11–18 — BuildCreate branded mobile hub, task pathways, chemical register and starter trackers.
Days 19–24 — Customer ReviewCustomer checks links, ownership, document status and wording. No unapproved instructions go live.
Days 25–30 — PilotSmall user group tests navigation. Capture feedback and agreed next actions.
Low-risk offer: Start with one area rather than asking a small company to replace its whole H&S system.
